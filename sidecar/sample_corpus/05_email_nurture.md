Email nurture bridges the gap between first touch and sales readiness. The goal is education, not pitching.

## Sequence design

A five-email sequence over two weeks works for most B2B consideration cycles. Start with the problem, move to frameworks for evaluating solutions, then introduce your approach — in that order.

## Subject lines and open rates

Short subject lines (4-7 words) that promise a specific insight outperform clever or vague ones. Test two variants per send. Open rates below 25% signal a list quality or deliverability problem, not a subject line problem.

## Personalization that matters

Segment by industry or role, not just name. "Hi {first_name}" adds nothing. "Here's how other B2B SaaS teams handle this" tells the reader you understand their context.
