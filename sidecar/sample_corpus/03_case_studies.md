Case studies convert when they mirror the reader's situation. Lead with the customer's problem, not your product name.

## Structure that works

Open with a one-sentence summary of the outcome. Follow with the situation before your product, the specific actions taken, and measurable results. Close with a quote from the customer that reinforces the transformation.

## Quantify everything

"Reduced time-to-publish by 60%" beats "significantly faster." Vague claims read as marketing fluff. If the customer cannot share exact numbers, use ranges or relative improvements.

## Match to funnel stage

Decision-stage readers already know the category. They need proof that your product works for someone like them. Lead with industry, company size, and use case — not feature lists.
